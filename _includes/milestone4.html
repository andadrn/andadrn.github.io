Interviews:

We interviewed 5 persons. 4 of them are supposed to be clients, the other being the worker. All 5 of them were interested in using our application so we thought we could get a better idea of how to improve our design by talking to them.

Client 1:

Q: How many times a month you require daily workers? (plumbers, babysitters etc.)

A: 3

Q: How do you usually reach daily workers when needed?

A: By phone after finding them on lajumate.ro.

Q: Do you ask recommendations via Facebook or other websites for daily workers?

A: No.

Q: Have you encountered problems when working with a daily worker? How did you solve them?

A: No.

Q: How many times a month would you consider using our application?:

A: 5

Q: How would you expect the process of reaching a daily worker to take place?

A: By text, with the possibility to easily send a location.

Q: Would you want the history of matches to be kept in order to reach out to the same workers?

A: Yes, especially if their work was exceptional.

Q: What would be the decisive factor that will make you continue using our application?

A: A reasonable pool of available workers.

Client 3:

Q: How many times a month you require daily workers? (plumbers, babysitters etc.)

A: 0

Q: How do you usually reach daily workers when needed?

A: I google it.

Q: Do you ask recommendations via Facebook or other websites for daily workers?

A: No.

Q: Have you encountered problems when working with a daily worker? How did you solve them?

A: The worker didn’t do his job perfectly, so I had to call him again and finish the job in a proper manner.

Q: How many times a month would you consider using our application?

A: As many times as it takes, I am quite tired of trying to reach out to such workers by myself without having the certainty that they will eventually come and fix my problem.

Q: How would you expect the process of reaching a daily worker to take place?

A: I would like to be able to select my problem, input my address and be found by one of the workers. I would also want to be able to tell if the worker is serious and usually does a good job. I would not want my problems to turn into something worse.

Q: Would you want the history of matches to be kept in order to reach out to the same workers?

A: I think that would be useful. I would like to collaborate with a good worker again.

Q: What would be the decisive factor that will make you continue using our application?

A: As previously stated, as long as the work gets done quickly and properly, I will keep on using it.

Client 4:

Q: How many times a month you require daily workers? (plumbers, babysitters etc.)

A: Almost never. 3-4 times a year.

Q: How do you usually reach daily workers when needed?

A: I ask a friend if he knows someone who can help me. Then I search online.

Q: Do you ask recommendations via Facebook or other websites for daily workers?

A: Rarely.

Q: Have you encountered problems when working with a daily worker? How did you solve them?

A: I did not, but if I did, I would simply talk to them.

Q: How many times a month would you consider using our application?

A: Every time I need someone to take care of my problems, which means that has to happen fast.

Q: How would you expect the process of reaching a daily worker to take place?

A: I would like the process to be really simple. Probably I will have a profile page and all I would have to do is choose one particular problem and appear in a list of clients from which workers can choose from. The worker would see the addresses and choose carefully in order to be there really quick. Also I would like to be able to say no to a worker, if he previously did poor work. Thus, I think a rating system would be useful.

Q: Would you want the history of matches to be kept in order to reach out to the same workers?

A: Not necessarily, but I think it could help if you ever want to choose a particular worker for some similar job.

Q: What would be the decisive factor that will make you continue using our application?

A: Good and quick work from the matching workers.

Worker 1:

Q: How many times a month a client reaches you?

A: It depends.

Q: How does a client reach you?

A: They call me.

Q: Do you use Facebook or other websites to find clients?

A: Yes, I usually post on Facebook groups.

Q: Have you encountered problems when working with a client? How did you solve them?

A: No.

Q: How many times a month would you consider using our application?

A: As often as possible, since I am not quite able to find work myself. My hope is I will be more efficient in my activity when the application gets launched.

Q: How would you expect the process of reaching a client to take place?

A: I would like to see lists of open jobs where the address is very obvious so I can also optimize my routes and be at the clients on time.

Q: Would you want the history of matches to be kept?

A: From my point of view, not necessarily, but I do not think it would be a problem if that is kept.

Q: What would be the decisive factor that will make you continue using our application?

A: I expect to find jobs quickly. If that does not happen, I will probably look for a better option. But after I manage to solve certain people's problems, maybe that will make them become regulars.

User Persona:

  • Name: Alex
  • Age: 27
  • Status: employed in the corporate environment
  • Education: graduated a university
  • Location: based in Bucharest
  • Mannerisms: modern, passionate about technology, socially active, "time is money" driven person
  • Issues: little time to spend outside work, needs to automate certain processes in his day-to-day life

User stories resulted from the interviews:

Client:

  • would like to rate a worker's performance (1-5 stars?)
  • would like to refuse a worker based on his rating

Worker:

  • would prefer to have a list of clients to be displayed to choose a job based on the address in order to optimize the routes

User flows:

Client: Registers -> fills in profile details when he/she has a problem -> picks a problem from a list and is inserted into a list that the workers can see -> is matched to a worker -> accepts/denies -> can rate the worker.

Worker: Registers -> fills his job type -> chooses a client from a list -> can be rated
